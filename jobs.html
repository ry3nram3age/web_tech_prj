Cybersecurity Specialist

Brief Description

  • Job Title: Cybersecurity Specialist
  • Company: ChickenJockey's CyberSec
  • Location: Melbourne HQ
  • Salary Range: $90,000 - $130,000 per year (based on experience)
  • Job Reference ID: 00001

Job Description:

The Cybersecurity Specialist at ChickenJockey's CyberSec will play a key role in safeguarding our clients' digital assets by identifying vulnerabilities, implementing security protocols, and responding to incidents. You will work with advanced technologies to ensure robust security across systems and networks.

Key Job Responsibilities:

  • Perform vulnerability assessments and penetration testing.
  • Lead incident response and security breach resolution.
  • Design and implement secure networks and systems.
  • Monitor security alerts and proactively defend against cyber threats.
  • Conduct security audits and provide recommendations for improvements.

Qualifications Required:

Essential:
  • 3+ years of hands-on cybersecurity experience.
  • Strong knowledge of network protocols, firewalls, and security tools.
  • Certifications: CISSP, CEH, CompTIA Security+, or equivalent.
  • Experience with security platforms like Splunk, Nessus, or Wireshark.
  • Excellent problem-solving and communication skills.
Preferable:
  • Experience in a Security Operations Center (SOC).
  • Advanced certifications (e.g., CISM, CCSP, OSCP).
  • Programming knowledge (Python, PowerShell, Bash).
  • Experience with cloud security (AWS, Azure, GCP).

Languages Required:

  • English (fluent, written and spoken).
  • Additional languages (Spanish, Mandarin) are a plus.

Investigation Team leader

Brief Description

  • Job Title: Investigation Team Leader
  • Company: ChickenJockey's's CyberSec
  • Location: Melbourne HQ
  • Salary Range: $95,000 - $130,000 per year (based on experience)
  • Job Reference ID: 00002

Job Description:

The Investigation Leader will be responsible for overseeing cybersecurity investigations related to incident response, digital forensics, and threat analysis. They will lead a team to identify and mitigate cybersecurity threats, while ensuring the integrity of digital evidence and maintaining a high standard of investigative practices. The role requires strong leadership skills and the ability to communicate findings effectively to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Key Job Responsibilities:

  • Lead and manage cybersecurity investigations, including incident response, digital forensics, and threat analysis.
  • Collaborate with internal cybersecurity teams to identify, mitigate, and prevent security risks.
  • Analyze and document findings, providing detailed reports and recommendations for remediation.
  • Ensure proper handling, collection, and preservation of digital evidence in compliance with legal standards.
  • Communicate findings clearly to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.
  • Mentor junior investigators, providing training and guidance in best practices.
  • Stay updated on the latest cybersecurity trends, threats, and technologies.

Qualifications Required:

Essential:
  • Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, IT, or a related field.
  • At least 5 years of experience in cybersecurity, with a focus on incident response or digital forensics.
  • Proven experience leading or managing investigation teams.
  • Strong knowledge of cybersecurity frameworks, tools, and standards.
  • Excellent problem-solving, analytical, and communication skills.
Preferable:
  • Master's degree in Cybersecurity or a related field.
  • Relevant certifications such as CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker), CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional), or CCFP (Certified Cyber Forensics Professional).
  • Experience with cloud security and multi-cloud environments.
  • Familiarity with malware analysis and reverse engineering techniques.

Languages Required:

  • English (fluent, both written and spoken).
  • Additional languages (e.g., Spanish, French, Mandarin) would be a plus but are not mandatory.
